# Antireflective Coatings

This folder contains a comprehensive academic presentation and technical study on the physics and engineering of antireflective (AR) coatings. The project was developed for the "Waves and Guides" (Guias e Ondas) course at the Federal University of Ceará (UFC).

## 📋 Overview
Uncoated glass surfaces typically reflect about 4% of incident light per interface, leading to cumulative transmission losses in complex optical systems and solar panels. This study explores the electromagnetic principles used to mitigate these losses through destructive interference.

## 🎯 Key Concepts Explored
* **Fresnel Equations & Snell's Law:** Foundational analysis of light behavior at dielectric interfaces.
* **Quarter-Wave Coating:** Mathematical proof and modeling of ideal single-layer coatings using phase and amplitude conditions.
* **Brewster's Angle:** Analysis of oblique incidence and polarization (TE/TM waves).
* **Multilayer Systems:** Implementation of the Transfer Matrix Method to model broadband AR coatings (e.g., Al2O3 + MgF2 V-coats).
* **Nanomanufacturing:** Overview of deposition techniques such as Sol-Gel, PECVD, and Sputtering.

## 📂 Contents
* `Presentation.pdf`: The complete slide deck (in Portuguese) detailing the theoretical derivations, mathematical models, and graphical simulations of reflectance vs. wavelength/angle.
* `source_code.tex`: The LaTeX source code utilized to typeset the mathematical formulations and structure the academic presentation.
